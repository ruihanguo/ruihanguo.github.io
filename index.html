Hello

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Materials Science and Engineering at the University of California, Berkeley, exploring device architectures for recording time-dependent stimuli without continuous power consumption.

I received my Ph.D. in Materials Science and Engineering from UC Berkeley in 2024, advised by Prof. Junqiao Wu, and my M.Eng. also from UC Berkeley. I earned my B.Eng. in Materials Science and Engineering at Tianjin University, together with a dual B.Fin. from Nankai University.

Before returning to academia, I led plasmonic near-field transducer integration for heat-assisted magnetic recording (HAMR) at Western Digital, and earlier served as Principal Engineer and R&D Lead at Quick Flash Technologies, developing electrochromic smart-window technologies that are now commercially deployed.

My work brings together nanofabrication, multiphysics simulation, and machine learning to build materials systems that sense, compute, and remember — from voltage-free in-memory sensors to all-optical neural networks and thermally adaptive photonic coatings.

Research Interests

  • Mem-sensing devices — voltage-free, in-memory sensors exploiting built-in electric fields at solid–liquid interfaces.
  • Ex-situ signal reconstruction — recovering time-dependent signals from irreversible material transformations, using coupled COMSOL + deep neural networks.
  • All-optical neural networks — liquid-crystal / CMOS hybrid microdevice arrays for nonlinear optical computation.
  • Adaptive thermal materials — phase-change and photonic coatings (TARC, VO₂ systems) for passive thermal management.
  • Plasmonic & near-field devices — plasmonic near-field transducers for HAMR and related nanophotonic integration.
